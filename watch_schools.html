U.S. Watchmaking Institutes

Lititz Watch Technicum

1 Springs Drive, Lititz, PA 17543

Founded by Rolex in 2001, Lititz Watch Technicum provides a highly selective two-year program in watchmaking, following the Swiss curriculum and preparing students for the SAWTA certification.

Watch Technology Institute at North Seattle College

9600 College Way N, Seattle, WA 98103

This program offers comprehensive training in mechanical and quartz watch repair and restoration. Students earn a certificate after two years, with the option to complete an Associate degree.

Swiss Watchmaking School (WOSTEP USA)

Miami, FL

WOSTEP USA is the American extension of the renowned Swiss watchmaking program. It offers advanced certification for professionals and aspiring horologists with intensive coursework and hands-on instruction.

Bulova School of Watchmaking (Historic)

New York, NY (Historic)

Though no longer operating, the Bulova School of Watchmaking trained generations of American watchmakers after WWII, and was a pioneer in accessible horological education, especially for veterans.

Paris Junior College - Watchmaking Program

2400 Clarksville Street, Paris, TX 75460

This Texas-based program offers state-of-the-art facilities and a 2-year curriculum that includes watch repair, micro-mechanics, and timekeeping restoration, preparing students for AWCI certification.

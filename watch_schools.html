U.S. Watchmaking Institutes

Lititz Watch Technicum

1 Keystone Court, Lititz, PA 17543

The Lititz Watch Technicum, founded by Rolex in 2001, offers an intensive two-year program designed to prepare students for careers in high-end watch servicing and repair. The curriculum includes micromechanics, movement service, and casing techniques.

Nicholas G. Hayek Watchmaking School

726 Exchange Street Suite 825, Buffalo, NY 14210

Operated by the Swatch Group, this school provides WOSTEP-certified training in traditional and modern watchmaking. The program includes 3,000 hours of hands-on education, preparing students for a wide range of horological careers.

Watch Technology Institute at North Seattle College

9600 College Way N, Seattle, WA 98103

This two-year Associate of Applied Science degree program offers an intensive curriculum in mechanical and quartz watch repair. WTI is known for its strong ties to the watch industry and exceptional graduate placement rates.

Bulova School of Watchmaking (Historical)

29-10 Thomson Ave, Long Island City, NY 11101

Originally established to train WWII veterans, the Bulova School was instrumental in U.S. horological education for decades. Though no longer operational, its legacy paved the way for modern watchmaking programs across the country.
